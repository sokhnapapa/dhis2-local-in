The QuIC Scorecard uses data collected by brief telephone interviews with facilities to determine readiness to provide quality EmONC services. It gathers data about the seven enablers for providing EmONC services and the presence of these enablers either during the last 24 hours or at the point in time of the phone call. It also asks direct questions about ability to perform the signal functions.

Performance Rating Score Facility has:
  Green   Met all items
  Yellow 3 Met all but 1 item
  Orange 2 Met >50% of items
  Red        1 < 50% of items

The brief QuIC interview cannot give a complete a picture of quality of care at each facility assessed. However QuIC can flag issues for further investigation at health facility or district level. Lack of enabling factors will also affect a facility’s ability to provide other basic services.

Please note the criteria to achieve each enabler are different according to whether a service is designated as Basic or Comprehensive EmONC (BEmONC or CEmONC), and whether a facility has a laboratory or not. Enabler question responses are used to cross-check answers to signal function questions.

Ability to perform signal function

No Not able to perform signal function

QuIC then asks a series of questions to create a snapshot of the number of EmONC ‘signal functions’ a facility is able to provide at the time of the call, an adaptation of the ‘last 3 months’ question usually asked in EmONC surveys.

Health workers often improvise to provide EmONC signal functions when supplies and equipment are unavailable. However QuIC only considers quality provision of a signal function to be available if provision is safe for the patient and safe for the health worker

EmONC status Facility has:
CEmONC Able to perform Comprehensive Emergency and Neonatal Care signal functions
BEmONC Able to perform Basic Emergency and Neonatal Care signal functions
Blank Unable to perform sufficient signal functions to achieve Emergency Obstetric Care status

QuIC also assigns an EmONC status to each facility, based on the number of signal functions a facility can provide.

For more details, please contact Evidence for Action Malawi QUIC.Malawi@options.co.uk
