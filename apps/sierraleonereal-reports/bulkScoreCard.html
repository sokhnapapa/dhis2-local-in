The Safer Clinic Scorecard uses data collected by brief telephone interviews with facilities to determine readiness to provide quality EmONC services. It complements the more resource intensive FIT assessments, providing data to track EmONC status at points in time between FIT visits. It gathers data about the same seven enablers for providing EmONC services as the FIT, but focuses on the presence of enablers either during the last 24 hours or at the point in time of the phone call. It also asks direct questions about ability to perform the signal functions.

The Safer Clinic interview has fewer enabler questions than FIT and cannot give so complete a picture of quality of care at each facility assessed. It is possible; therefore, that FIT and Safer Clinic traffic light scoring might not always be exactly the same. The enabler questions which remain in this scorecard provide a means to verify responses to signal function questions (described below).

Please note the criteria to achieve each enabler are different for hospitals and CHCs because CHCs are not expected to perform complicated deliveries that need instrumental or operative procedures.

The Safer Clinic interview asks a series of spot-check questions to assess a facility’s ability to perform EmONC signal function services within the next hour, an adaptation of the ‘last 3 months’ question usually asked in EmONC surveys.

Spot-check answers are cross-checked against responses from enabler questions to determine whether a signal function can be performed.

EmONC status Facility has:
CEmONC Able to perform Comprehensive Emergency and Neonatal Care signal functions
BEmONC Able to perform Basic Emergency and Neonatal Care signal functions
Blank Unable to perform sufficient signal functions to achieve Emergency Obstetric Care status

This scorecard assesses EmONC status based on responses to signal function questions.

This is in contrast to the FIT which assessed EmONC status based on enabler questions only and does not include questions related to signal functions e.g. assisted vaginal delivery.
